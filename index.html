I am a Ph.D. candidate in Economics at Howard University. My work in applied microeconomics and econometrics examines how energy and climate change policies influence urban resilience, energy efficiency, and equity outcomes. I combine econometric and machine learning methods to study household energy use, building performance standards, and policy impacts in U.S. cities.

With over eight years of experience in research and policy analysis, I have worked across the private sector and development organizations, including the World Bank Group.

My dissertation, "Climate Change, Energy Demand, and Policy Impacts: Analyzing Residential and Building Energy Dynamics in the U.S.", investigates household and building energy responses to climate change and regulation.
