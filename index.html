I am a Ph.D. candidate in Economics at Howard University. My research examines how climate change and energy policies shape urban resilience, energy efficiency, and equity outcomes. I apply econometric and machine learning methods to analyze household energy use, building performance standards, and policy effects in U.S. cities.

My dissertation, "Climate Change, Energy Demand, and Policy Impacts: Analyzing Residential and Building Energy Dynamics in the U.S.", investigates household and building energy responses to climate stress and regulation.
