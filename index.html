Job Market Paper

Building Energy Performance Standards: Impacts on Building Energy Efficiency and GHG Emissions in Washington, DC

Evaluates the causal impacts of Washington, DC’s Building Energy Performance Standards (BEPS), a pioneering U.S. policy requiring underperforming buildings to improve energy efficiency. Using panel data from 2012–2023, the paper estimates BEPS effects on energy use intensity and GHG emissions while exploring heterogeneity across ownership types. Results indicate substantial improvements in efficiency and emissions outcomes, particularly among low-performing buildings, offering new empirical evidence for the policy’s role in advancing urban decarbonization.

Climate Change, Energy Demand, and Policy Impacts: Analyzing Residential and Building Energy Dynamics in the U.S.

Investigates how climate change intensifies household and building energy demand while assessing the effectiveness of performance-based energy policies. Combines distributional econometrics, causal ML, and panel causal inference to generate evidence for equitable energy transitions.
